SMALL SCHOOLS. BIG IDEAS.

We want to prove that small schools can do big things.

We’re a team of student organizers from Philly’s three best liberal arts colleges - Swarthmore, Haverford, and Bryn Mawr. For the first time, we’re bringing together 100 new and experienced hackers from our schools for a weekend of hacking.

Our goal is an inclusive space for people of all backgrounds and majors to work on amazing projects for a weekend. Seasoned hackers in top CS programs will mentor novice hackers as they team up to create web, mobile, and hardware projects from the ground-up. In bringing students together right before the presidential election, Hack the TriCo will also be an avenue to encourage civic engagement through tech. A series of workshops throughout the weekend will help hackers learn to use specific platforms and get started on their projects.

The liberal arts are often overlooked in the tech world. Hack the TriCo will give liberal arts students the chance to combine our technical expertise with our creativity, analytical skills, and interdisciplinary thinking. At Hack the TriCo, small schools will bring big ideas to the table.

FAQ

What is a Hackathon?
A hackathon is an event where students come together and dedicate themselves to a project for a weekend with a team of friends or strangers. You bring your ideas. We supply the hardware, the software, the coffee, the food and mentorship needed to make your ideas a reality.
Where is Hack The TriCo?
Hack The TriCo is going to be held at Haverford College-- one of the three Tri-College liberal arts colleges (alongside Swarthmore College and Bryn Mawr College) in the Greater Philadelphia area.
Who can attend?
We welcome any college student to come to Hack The TriCo. We are working on travel reimbursement and we'll keep you posted.
